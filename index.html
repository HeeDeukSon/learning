The Language Learning Doctrine: 9 Principles

Adapted from David Heinemeier Hansson's "The Rails Doctrine"

1. Optimize for Learner Enjoyment.

We prioritize making language learners genuinely enjoy the process, rather than solely optimizing for speed or perfect grammar. Joy fuels consistent progress.

2. Convention Over Customization.

We advocate for sensible, common learning paths and resources, reducing the need for constant manual setup or endless searching for the "perfect" method.

3. The Curriculum is Omakase.

We provide curated, opinionated learning choices, freeing learners from being overwhelmed by endless options and guiding them effectively.

4. No One Methodology.

We embrace multiple learning approaches‚Äîbe it immersion, grammar study, or speaking practice‚Äîinstead of dogmatically adhering to just one. Flexibility is key.

5. Exalt Beautiful Communication.

We value clear, elegant expression that treats language as an art form, encouraging learners to communicate effectively and gracefully.

6. Provide Sharp Tools and Optimized Input.

We offer powerful learning tools, focusing on those that deliver comprehensible and compelling input (and opportunities for compelling output), even if they can be misused.

7. Value Integrated Systems and Authentic Experiences.

We favor holistic approaches where different skills (reading, writing, listening, speaking) and resources work together seamlessly, actively incorporating authentic, real-life materials and experiences into the learning journey.

8. Progress Over Absolute Perfection.

We prioritize continuous improvement and effective communication over achieving flawless grammar or accent immediately. Every step forward is a victory.

9. Push Up a Big Tent.

We welcome diverse learners and learning styles, embracing all motivations for language acquisition, not just academic elites or those aiming for native fluency. Everyone is welcome.
